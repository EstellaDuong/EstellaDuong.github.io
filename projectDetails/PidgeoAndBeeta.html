Summary

This was my senior capstone project. I co-developed a 2D cooperative puzzle-platformer in Godot, featuring asymmetric co-op gameplay where a pigeon and beetle use complementary abilities to solve environmental puzzles. Outside of level design and development, I primarily illustrated all original 2d assets for the game. This includes character designs and animations, background art, props, tilesets, and cutscenes. I also designed tile-based level layouts and interconnected puzzles that support a narrative-driven story of betrayal and revenge. This was developed with a 4-person team plus collaborating with an outside composer for original music.

Tilesets

As the sole artist for this project, I created all tilesets used in the game, ensuring a consistent visual style throughout the levels. One of the most challenging aspects of this was finding that drawing traditional tilesets with 47 or more combinations required a lot of time. Then, I discovered a method called the "dual-grid system," and quickly looked into how we could implement it into our game. In short, the dual-grid system uses one "world grid" and one "offset" grid. The second grid is offset by half a tile, and an algorithm is used to determine what each of its four corners will look like, reducing the number of tile combinations to just 16. With this method, I was able to optimize my time and create multiple tilesets to fill out the environment.
