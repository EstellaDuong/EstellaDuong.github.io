Summary

Pidgeo & Beeta came about as my senior capstone project, where I worked in a 4-person team and collaborated with an outside music composer. I co-developed this 2D cooperative puzzle-platformer in Godot. Our game features asymmetric gameplay where two players take on the role of a pigeon (Pidgeo) and beetle (Beeta) duo. Players must use complementary abilities to solve environmental puzzles and progress through levels, developing the story along the way. Outside of level design and development, I primarily illustrated original 2d assets for the game. This includes character designs and animations, background art, props, tilesets, and cutscenes. The final game was presented at ATLAS Expo 2026.

Pidgeo & Beeta opens in Feather Hollow, a once peaceful village, when it is turned upside down after a brutal attack and mass kidnapping at the hands of Pidgeo's brother. An unlikely pair, Pidgeo and Beeta, must work together across treacherous landscapes. The two animals must solve puzzles and uncover clues to reveal the truth behind the tragedy and exact their revenge.

Tilesets

As the sole artist for this project, I created all tilesets used in the game, ensuring a consistent visual style throughout the levels. One of the most challenging aspects of this was that drawing traditional tilesets with 47 or more combinations required a lot of time. When I discovered a method called the "dual-grid system," I quickly looked into how we could implement it into our game. In short, the dual-grid system uses one "world grid" and one "offset" grid. The second grid is offset by half a tile, and an algorithm is used to determine what each of its four corners will look like, reducing the number of tile combinations to just 16. With this method, I was able to optimize my time and create multiple tilesets to fill out the environment.
