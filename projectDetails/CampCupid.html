Summary

This dating simulation game was developed in Godot, featuring original art assets and a physical bow-and-arrow controller to create a unique, arcade-style game. I worked in a 3-person team to develop this game for a class called Alternative Arcade Interfaces. This was an interesting class focused on creating "alternative" or weird, never-before-seen controller inputs and experiences. Our prototype had the opportunity to be exhibited at Whaaat!? Festival 2025.

This game initially started with the concept of playing as Cupid and shooting love arrows at potential matches. Then, our idea turned into players partaking in their own love story, where they must use the bow and arrow controller to shoot dialog boxes and score in minigames, all while trying to avoid accidentally shooting their favorite characters. Will their aiming skills be enough to score a date to the camp prom by the end of their 3-day stay? The premise of the game, paired with engaging visuals and a real bow-and-arrow controller lead to some hilarious moments and a memorable experience.

Bow-And-Arrow Controller

The Camp Cupid controller was built from a modified children's bow fitted with a custom-designed 3D-printed handle to house the electronics. We used an IR Wii Remote camera for aiming and tracking, a trigger button for secondary gameplay input, and a TinyCore microcontroller to process inputs, detect impacts, and wirelessly communicate with the game. We also included a padded PVC pipe for the arrow to travel through, which added safety and physical "hit" feedback. One issue we came across was that the recoil when the bow was fired caused the reticule to jump slightly, causing players to miss. To fix this, all we had to do was add a delay to the stored position tracking. By taking the aimed position a few milliseconds prior to firing, we could register where players were meaning to aim without the recoil causing inaccuracies.
